Course Overview

The SALOWN School Level 2 diploma in Beauty Services is is a practical and knowledge-based qualification. It is aimed at equipping therapists with skills required for work in salons, destination/day spas and cruise ships. This qualifications will qualify learners to become a beauty therapist and allows learners to gather evidence in a realistic working environment or real working environment without the need for fee paying clients

The course have been classified into 3 units which are PPCM, Major Application and Knowledge to learn.

Units

Professional Pattern of Communication Management(PPCM)

  • Meet, Greet, Impression (MGI String)
  • Exceptional client consultation (XCON)
  • Dealing with difficult situations and unhappy clients (Risilence Strategy)

Major Application

  • Implement and maintain safe, hygienic and effective working practices
  • Consult, assess, plan and prepare for procedures
  • Follow emergency procedure
  • Carry out salon reception duties
  • Advance face massage techniques
  • Provide advance treatment for skin types
    1. Oily
    2. Dry
    3. Sensitive
    4. Balanced
    5. Combination
  • Provide advance treatment for skin conditions
    1. Lax elasticity
    2. Hyper and hypo pigmentation
    3. Congested
    4. Pustular
    5. Fragile
    6. Vascular
    7. Sensitized
    8. Dehydrated
    9. Photo-sensitive
    10. Photo-aged
    11. Mature
    12. Lack lustre
  • Provide advance depilation/waxing services
  • Sugar strip wax, Hot wax and Warm wax
  • Provide advanced manicure treatment
  • Provide advanced pedicure treatment
  • Enhance eyebrow and eyelashes
    1. Strip artificial eyelashes
    2. Flare artificial eyelashes
    3. Individual single eyelashes
  • Prepare and provide makeup

Knowledge to learn

  • Treatment for minors
  • Skin Anatomy and Physiology
  • Structure and function of skin
  • Skin analysis
    1. Skin classification
    2. Skin type
    3. Skin condition
  • Bones of head, neck, girdle, shoulder girdle and chest
  • Position of head, neck, chest and shoulder girdle
  • pH scale and its relevance to skin sensitivity
  • Contra-indications
  • Galvanic
  • High frequency
  • Ultrasonic
  • Skin Massage techniques
  • Nail anatomy
  • Nail shapes
  • Nail analysis
    1. Nail classification
    2. Nail type
    3. Nail condition
  • Nail massage medium
  • Nail massage techniques
  • Application protocol
Learning Outcomes

The SALOWN School Level 2 Diploma in Beauty Therapy provides Learners with the knowledge and skills to carry out the recognised beauty treatments in today’s marke

On successful completion of SALOWN School Level 2 Diploma in Beauty Therapy, the learners will be able to:

  • provide Learners with a challenging qualification that will enable them to gain the necessary knowledge and skills for work as a beauty therapist.
  • provide commercial skills in facial skincare, waxing techniques, manicure treatments, pedicure treatments, make-up and one additional skill selected from the optional units : thermal auricular therapy treatments, eyelash and eyebrow treatments or gel manicure services
  • encourage wider learning of new treatments on the market and the CPD opportunities available on completion of the course
  • provide Learners with the opportunity to recognise the changing industry and be able to explore further avenues that will enhance skills in the future
  • provide Learners with the tools and terminology required to work within the beauty industry
  • enable Learners to make informed and confident decisions relating to Clients’ needs and requirements
  • provide knowledge of the health and safety requirements surrounding the beauty industry
  • provide opportunities for Learners to develop the necessary skills and competencies to move on to further study or employment
  • encourage a deeper understanding of ‘effective communication’ with Clients and team members
Entry Requirements
  • Learners should either be working in or aspiring to join the Beauty Industry if possible. They should ideally have:
    1. a good general education, with a minimum qualification of 10th pass, including Mathematics and English. This may include achievement of Functional Skills or Essential Skills in Mathematics and English
